## Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the bike sales data to understand customer demographics, income levels, and commuting patterns that influence purchasing decisions. An interactive Excel dashboard was developed to convert raw customer data into clear and meaningful business insights.

---

## Project Objective
The primary goals of this project are:
- Identify which customer segments are more likely to purchase bikes  
- Analyze the impact of income, age, and commute distance on buying behavior  
- Provide data-driven insights to improve marketing and sales strategies

## Key Insights

### Income & Purchase Behavior
- Customers who purchased bikes generally have **higher average incomes** than non-buyers.  
- Male buyers show **slightly higher income averages** compared to female buyers.  
- Purchases are most common among **middle-to-high income groups**.

### Commute Distance Impact
- Customers with **0‚Äì1 mile commute distance** have the **highest purchase rate**.  
- Bike purchases **decrease as commute distance increases**.  
- Commutes **above 10 miles** show the **lowest likelihood of bike purchases**.

### Age Bracket Trends
- The **Middle Age group (31‚Äì50 years)** represents the **largest buyer segment**.  
- Young adults and older age groups show comparatively **lower purchase counts**.

### Gender Distribution
- Male customers slightly lead in total purchases.  
- The difference is small, meaning **both genders are strong potential markets**.

### Education & Region Observations
- Higher education levels tend to align with **more stable purchasing behavior**.  
- Regional filters reveal **noticeable variations** in buying trends.

## Conclusion
The analysis shows that **income level, commute distance, and age group** are the most influential factors in bike purchasing decisions. Middle-aged individuals with moderate to high incomes and shorter commute distances are the most likely customers.

Businesses can use these insights to:
- Target **working professionals** with focused campaigns  
- Promote bikes as **short-distance commuting solutions**  
- Offer **discounts or installment plans** for younger audiences  
- Apply **customer segmentation** for personalized marketing
